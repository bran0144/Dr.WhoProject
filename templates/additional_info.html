Most Iconic Places to visit as a Whovian

The Shard

From The Bells of St John episode with 11th Doctor, Matt Smith. In this famous scene, the Doctor and Clara ride an anti-gravity motorbike up its side.

Link to Video

Visiting the Shard

London Eye

From the first episode with Chritopher Eccleston, "Rose", the Doctor discovers that the Nestene Consciousness was using the London Eye as a transmitter. The 11th Doctor also flies past the Eye, clinging by his fingers to the Tardis's doorway.

Link to Video

Visiting the London Eye

Shakespeare's Globe

From the episode in season three, "The Shakespeare Code", the Doctor inspires Shakespeare while also saving him from alien witches, Carrionites. He famously quotes JK Rowling to close the portal and imprison the witches.

Link to Video

Visiting the Globe

Buckingham Palace

From the Christmas special, "Voyage of the Damned". The Doctor prevents a Titanic shaped spaceship from colliding with Buckingham Palace.

Link to Video

Visiting Buckingham Palace

Trafalgar Square

From the "Day of the Doctor".

Link to Video

Ragley Hall

If "The Girl in the Fireplace" is your favorite episode, then this is a must stop. The ballroom featured in the episode is from the interior of this beautiful home.

Link to Video

Visit Ragley Hall

Battersea Power Station

Featured in many classic Who episodes, including with the First Doctor, but also used in the "Rise of the Cybermen" episode.

Link to Video

Earl's Court Tardis

You can't go in to find out if it's bigger on the inside, but you can take your picture with a Tardis at Earl's Court Station.

Visiting Earl's Court

The Who Shop

If you're in London and a Whovian, this is a must stop. They have a screen-used Tardis, 120 different props and costumes, and every single piece of merch you can think of.

Visit the Who Shop

Brit Movie Tours

If you want a guided tour, Brit Movie Tours offers many options, particularly in Cardiff, where the majority of the modern series is filmed.

Link to Tours

Riverside Studios

For the classic Who fan, this is the original studio where it was filmed until the 1970s. Now it is a theater, film studio, and arts space.

BBC Studios London

Tour the London Studio where the classic Who episodes of the 1970s and 1980s were filmed. Maybe even sign up to be in the audience for one of the live taped shows, like Later with Jools Holland.

Roath Lock Studios Cardiff

Unfortunately, Uppter Boat Studios (where the modern Who episodes were filmed from 2006-2012, along with Sherlock), is closed to the public and does not offer tours. Indoor Who filming is now at Roath Lock Studios in Cardiff along the water. Unfortunately, they do not offer public tours. You can tour BBC Cymru, but this is not a Doctor Who tour.
